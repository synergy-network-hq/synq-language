SynQ Trademark Usage Guidelines

“SynQ” and associated logos, names, and identifiers are trademarks of the SynQ project and its author(s).

1. Permitted Uses (No Approval Required)

You may use the “SynQ” name solely for descriptive purposes, including:

Referring to the SynQ programming language or VM

Discussing compatibility or research related to SynQ

Academic papers, blog posts, and documentation

Forks clearly labeled as unofficial

Example:

“This tool is inspired by SynQ.”

2. Restricted Uses (Approval Required)

You may not use the SynQ name or branding to:

Imply official endorsement or certification

Market a product as “SynQ-compatible,” “SynQ-certified,” or “Official SynQ”

Use SynQ logos in commercial branding

Represent a fork as the canonical implementation

Example (not allowed without permission):

“Enterprise-grade SynQ Runtime”

3. Fork Naming

Forks must clearly distinguish themselves.

Acceptable:

“MyCompany Quantum VM (derived from SynQ)”

Not acceptable:

“SynQ Enterprise Edition”

4. Certification Marks

Use of the terms “SynQ Certified,” “SynQ Verified,” or similar is reserved exclusively for participants in the official SynQ Certification Program.

These guidelines exist to protect ecosystem trust and developer clarity, not to restrict innovation.
